Frequently Asked Questions

Blockchain is a type of security technology that creates a permanent, unchangeable record of who accessed your health information and when. Think of it like a tamper-proof logbook. For your health records, this means you always have proof of who looked at your data, and no one can secretly change or delete that record.

Yes, you can remove access at any time. When you revoke access, the provider will no longer be able to view your records. Their past access is recorded in the blockchain audit log for your records, but they cannot see any new information after revocation.

During the 60-second countdown, nothing is finalized. Your data is not shared. The countdown is a safety feature that gives you time to change your mind. If you do nothing, the request is automatically cancelled. Only after you confirm does the access grant get recorded on the blockchain.

Your blockchain transactions (access grants and revocations) are recorded on a public ledger. The transactions contain only the record hash and provider address — not your actual medical data. Your medical data remains encrypted and stored separately. The blockchain ensures the audit trail cannot be tampered with.
