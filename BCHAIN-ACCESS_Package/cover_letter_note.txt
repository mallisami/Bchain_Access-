Scope and gating. We recognize that an editor may reasonably weigh two further options against the positioning above: gating acceptance on a minimal PwD pilot prior to publication, or requesting that the framework-and-taxonomy contribution and the implementation-and-evaluation contribution be split into two linked papers given the manuscript's length and cross-referencing density. Both are legitimate editorial paths that this paper's DSR framing does not foreclose; we have not pre-empted either by, for example, thinning the barrier taxonomy, the traceability matrix, or the reference-implementation detail, because we regard that technical material as necessary to the first-cycle artifact this paper reports rather than as padding. Our own preference, stated in Section sec:pwd-validation below, is to treat the pre-specified protocol as a companion study following this paper's acceptance, on the grounds that the standards-derived design contribution (RQ1--RQ3) is separable from, and does not require, PwD data to be evaluated on its own terms; we defer to editorial judgment on which path best serves the venue and its readers.
